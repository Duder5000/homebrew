Draconic Invocations

Wyrmling Invocations

  • Aquatic Adaptation:
    You can breathe air and water. You also gain a swim speed equal to your walk speed.
  • Beguiling Influence:
    You gain proficiency with Intimidation and Persuasion, if you already are already proficient you can double your proficiency bonus.
  • Breath of the Nigh:
    Once per short rest you can cast Darkness without expending a spell slot.
  • Scalding Gust:
    Once per long rest as an action you can create a strong blast of wind (as the Gust of Wind spell, except that the duration is instantaneous). Any creature within the effect takes fire damage equal to your proficiency modifier regardless of whether it succeeds or fails on the save.
  • Natural Armor:
    Well you are wearing no armor nor wielding a shield, your AC equals 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Charisma modifier.

Young Invocations

  • Boldly Talkative:
    You can cast Charm Monster once per long rest without extending a spell slot.
  • Draconic Flight:
    You gain a fly speed equal to twice your walk speed.
  • Voidsense:
    You again a 30ft blindsight.
  • Draconic Claws:
    When you make an attack with your claws they deal 1d10 damage instead of 1d8 damage. Additionally, when you make an attack with your claws you can choose to use the damage type of your breath weapon rather than Slashing.

Adult Invocations

  • Breath Aura:
    Whenever a creature within 5ft of you hits you with a melee attack, they take damage equal to your Charisma modifier, the damage type is the same as your breath weapon.
  • Wingstorm:
    Prerequisite: Draconic Flight or wings from another source
    Once per short rest as an action you can use your wings to create a storm of winds around you, pushing away and knocking down nearby creatures. All creatures within 20ft of you are pushed away until they are at least 25ft away and then knocked prone. A successful Strength save negates the effect; creatures more than one size category larger than you are immune. If an affected creature encounters an obstacle (such as a wall or another creature), both it and the obstacle each take 2d6 points of bludgeoning damage.
  • Terrifying Roar:
    You can cast the Fear spell without concentration once per long rest.
  • Ambushing Attack:
    You have advantage on an attack roll against a creature if at least one of your allies is within 5 feet of the creature and the ally isn’t incapacitated.

Ancient Invocations

  • Legendary Resistance:
    Once per day, if you fail a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.
  • Energy Immunity:
    You gain immunity to acid, cold, lightning, fire, or thunder damage. You choose the energy type when you transform.
  • Perilous Veil:
    Once per long rest as an action you can change the appearance of one or more creatures, as the Seeming spell. Any creature that succeeds on a Wisdom save to disbelieve (and thus see through) the glamour takes 5d6 points of psychic damage.
  • Fivefold Breath of Tiamat:
    Prerequisite: Chromatic Category
    Your breath weapon loses its normal energy type and you create five different breath weapons simultaneously: a cone of acid, a cone of cold, a cone of fire, a line of acid, and a line of lightning. Each breath weapon deals damage equal to half your normal breath weapon damage dice (rounded down), and each one allows a Dexterity save to halve the damage dealt. When you use this breath effect, you also take a number of points of damage equal to your warlock level, which can’t be lessened by damage reduction or any similar benefit.
  • Discorporating Breath of Bahamut:
    Prerequisite: Metallic Category
    Your breath weapon loses its normal energy type and becomes a blue-white line of pure energy. This breath effect doubles the number of dice of damage normally dealt by your breath weapon. In addition, any creature that is reduced to 0 or fewer hit points by the breath weapon is disintegrated (as the Disintegrate spell). A successful Constitution save halves the damage dealt. Unlike disintegrate, this breath effect has no effect on objects, even those made of force. When you use Discorporating Breath of Bahamut, you also take a number of points of damage equal to your warlock level, which can’t be lessened by damage reduction or any similar benefit.
  • Psycho-concussive Breath of Sardior
    Prerequisite: Gem Category
    Your breath weapon loses its normal energy type and becomes a colorless 15ft cone that deals Force damage equal to the normal number of dice dealt by your breath weapon. Additionally, any creature in the area must make an Intelligence save or take Psychic damage equal to the normal number of dice dealt by your breath weapon and become frightened for 1 minute. A creature can repeat the saving throw at the end of each of its turns, ending the effect on itself on a success. On a success they take no Psychic damage nor are they frightened. When you use Psycho-concussive Breath of Sardior, you also take a number of points of damage equal to your warlock level, which can’t be lessened by damage reduction or any similar benefit.
